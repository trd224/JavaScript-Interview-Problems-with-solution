What are the tradeoffs between using ngrx/effects vs raw RxJS Subjects for managing side effects in large Angular apps?

Advanced interview-level answer with headings, lists, full examples for every concept, colorful code, and expected behavior.

Interview-Level Answer

NgRx Effects

NgRx Effects are structured, testable, action-driven side-effect handlers. They work well in large Angular apps where state flow must be predictable and traceable.

Raw RxJS Subjects

Raw Subjects are lightweight and flexible, but they can become hard to debug and maintain when many components push events from different places.

Tradeoffs

NgRx EffectsStructured, testable, traceable, good for large teams and complex side effects.
RxJS SubjectsSimple, fast to implement, less boilerplate, good for small apps or local events.

Interview Point

For large apps, NgRx Effects provide better architecture. For small isolated cases, raw RxJS Subjects can be enough.

Key Points

  • NgRx Effects are better for complex side effects.
  • Subjects are lightweight but less structured.
  • Choose based on app size, complexity, team, and debugging needs.
Remember: NgRx Effects provide architecture; Subjects provide simplicity.

Full Understandable Example / Examples

ngrx-effect-example.ts
loadUsers$ = createEffect(() =>
  this.actions$.pipe(
    ofType(UserActions.loadUsers),
    switchMap(() =>
      this.http.get<User[]>('/api/users').pipe(
        map(users => UserActions.loadUsersSuccess({ users })),
        catchError(error =>
          of(UserActions.loadUsersFailure({ error }))
        )
      )
    )
  )
);
subject-side-effect-example.ts
private loadUsersSubject = new Subject<void>();

loadUsers$ = this.loadUsersSubject.pipe(
  switchMap(() => this.http.get('/api/users'))
);

loadUsers() {
  this.loadUsersSubject.next();
}

Expected Output / Behavior

NgRx effect is action-driven and traceable.
Subject approach is simpler but less structured.
